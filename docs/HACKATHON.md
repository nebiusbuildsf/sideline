## Problem Statements

### Statement 1: Edge Inference & Agents (OUR TRACK)
Build applications with Nebius Token Factory's open LLMs that go beyond tool calls — across software, robotics, and devices. Build agentic pipelines, robotics control loops that can reason over context, retrieve knowledge, plan next steps, and take actions. What can you build to improve agentic workflows or unlock new capabilities in real-world systems?

### Statement 2: Robotics Optimization, Training, & Tuning
Build a system that enables a robot to perceive its environment, reason about what's happening, and take meaningful physical actions. Can you train a policy with RL? How can a robot convert both visual and natural language input into actions? Can you develop any predictive world models to simulate future environments to inform decisions now?

Nebius AI Cloud Compute + supported simulation stack for motion generation, world models, and RL/policy training. Teams will train and test policies for navigation, locomotion, obstacle avoidance, reach-and-grasp, object handoff, and basic manipulation. Selected teams may get deployment time on shared fleet of 8-10 Unitree G1 Humanoid Robots (subject to safety review).

## Judging Criteria

| Criteria | Weight | What They Want |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| **Live Demo** | **45%** | Does it work live? How well implemented? How is it presented? |
| **Creativity & Originality** | **35%** | Has this been seen before? How unique in the field? Unique approach to problem statements? |
| **Impact Potential** | **20%** | Long-term potential? Lasting impact beyond hackathon? |

**They explicitly state:** "Show us what you have built to solve our problem statements. Please do not show us a presentation. We'll be checking to ensure your project was built entirely during the event."

### Judging Process
1. **Round 1:** Teams assigned groups of judges. ~3 minutes to pitch + 1-2 minutes Q&A
2. **Round 2:** Top 6 teams demo on stage to panel of judges. ~3 minutes to pitch + 2-3 minutes Q&A
